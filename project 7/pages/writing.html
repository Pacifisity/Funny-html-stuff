Writing Through Reading

Document

I am a reader learning how to write. In middle school when I got my first opportunity to freely choose what to read for my project, I fell in love with reading. I didn’t have a huge pool of books that I could choose from, and the books all related to the dire times of Nazi Germany, but I was satisfied with any freedom I could get. The teacher began to explain the due dates for when we were supposed to have read everything but at this point, I had tuned her out. I was basically focused on reading the first few pages of each book and making the most of my time. Eventually, one piqued my curiosity: Prisoner B-3087. In a few pages, I was already hooked and by the end of class, I was so lost in the book that I forgot about all of the other choices.

Aleks and Mental Health

Document

Three hours, that’s how long the pre-college math class was. Three hours might not sound like much; your average college student might even spend more than that binge watching their favorite show or scrolling TikTok. However, in an environment where hearing a pen drop would be almost ear-piercing… it wasn’t even comparable. Twice a week, I walked into the classroom and sat so close to other students and yet felt so far away. I have nearly completed the course and don’t need to attend the classes anymore. Not only have I completed it but I've done all 3 sections of 1099; I’ve already learned many of these concepts in the past. My high school transcripts were an absolute mess and I never took the SAT so I was put in the lowest level math classes available. Needless to say, I blew through them; despite that, it took five weeks for Aleks to place me where I needed to be. The novelty of a new school year was already wearing off at this point.
